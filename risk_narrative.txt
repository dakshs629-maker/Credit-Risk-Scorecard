# Credit Portfolio Risk Assessment: Income Segment Analysis

---

## Executive Summary

Analysis of the current credit portfolio across four income segments reveals materially divergent risk-return profiles, with two of the four segments generating negative net yield under prevailing pricing assumptions, raising significant concerns about portfolio-wide profitability and risk-adjusted return optimization. Segment 3 demonstrates the most favorable risk-return dynamic, posting a mean probability of default (PD) of 32.8% alongside a positive net yield of approximately 77.5 basis points, representing the sole high-conviction profitable segment at meaningful scale with 55,362 obligors. Segment 4 similarly generates positive, albeit compressed, net yield of approximately 46.6 basis points against a mean PD of 34.0% across 21,703 accounts, suggesting marginal but technically viable risk-adjusted economics under current spread assumptions. Critically, these two profitable segments together represent approximately 25.4% of total portfolio exposure by count, meaning the majority of booked volume is concentrated in segments that are actively destroying risk-adjusted value under current pricing.

---

## Risk Concentration and Loss Exposure

The two underperforming segments present compounding concerns from both a credit quality and portfolio sizing perspective. Segment 7, the largest single cohort at 158,774 obligors representing approximately 52.8% of total portfolio volume, carries the most elevated mean PD of 44.5% and delivers a deeply negative net yield of negative 410 basis points, indicating severe structural mispricing relative to the credit risk being absorbed. At this scale, the drag from Segment 7 alone is likely sufficient to render the aggregate portfolio unprofitable on a risk-adjusted basis, even accounting for the positive carry generated by Segments 3 and 4. Segment 1, the second largest cohort at 71,617 obligors, similarly posts a negative net yield of approximately negative 147 basis points against a mean PD of 38.8%, compounding the aggregate shortfall. The negative net yield figures in these segments imply that expected credit losses, net of interest income and applicable fees, are exceeding the revenue being generated — a dynamic that, if sustained, represents a forward-looking provisioning and capital adequacy concern that warrants immediate underwriting and pricing review
